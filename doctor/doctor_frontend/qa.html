What are the common side effects of chemotherapy for leukemia?
Published

Common side effects include fatigue, hair loss, nausea, vomiting, decreased blood cell counts, and increased risk of infection. Each patient may experience different side effects, and we monitor these closely during treatment.

Category: Side Effects | Last updated: Mar 2, 2025
How often should patients with leukemia have follow-up appointments after remission?
Published

During the first year of remission, patients typically have follow-up visits every 1-3 months. In the second year, visits may be scheduled every 2-4 months, and afterward, every 6-12 months. However, the frequency depends on the specific type of leukemia and individual risk factors.

Category: Follow-up Care | Last updated: Feb 25, 2025
Is exercise recommended during leukemia treatment?
Draft

Light to moderate exercise can be beneficial during leukemia treatment, depending on the patient's condition. It may help manage fatigue, maintain muscle mass, and improve mood. However, it's important to discuss with your healthcare team to develop an appropriate exercise plan.
